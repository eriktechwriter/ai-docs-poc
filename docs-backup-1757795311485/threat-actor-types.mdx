# Threat actor types

The table below describes the major types of threat actor groups:

- Advanced persistent threat (APT) attack groups
- Cybercriminals
- Hacktivists

<table>
<thead style="text-align: left;">
<tr>
<th><p>Characteristic</p></th>
<th><p>APT attack groups</p></th>
<th><p>Cybercriminals</p></th>
<th><p>Hacktivists</p></th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody style="text-align: left;">
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Motivation</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Strategic goals (espionage, data theft, infrastructure disruption)</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Financial gain</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Social, political, or ideological causes</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Sophistication</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Highly advanced, custom tools</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Varies, often use off-the-shelf malware</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Varies, often use publicly available tools</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Target selection</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Specific, high-value organizations or sectors</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Broad range of targets</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Organizations perceived as unethical or opposed to their cause</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Attack duration</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Long-term, persistent presence</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Generally shorter-term</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Usually short-term, event-driven</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Funding</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Well-funded, often state-sponsored</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Varies, self-funded to organized crime</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Limited, often self-funded</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Techniques</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Advanced, zero-day exploits, custom malware</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Known vulnerabilities, phishing, ransomware</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Distributed denial of service (DDoS) attacks, website defacement, information leaks</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Stealth</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>High emphasis on remaining undetected</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Varies, may prioritize quick payouts</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Often seek publicity for their actions</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Organization</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Highly organized teams</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Individual to organized groups</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Individual to loosely organized collectives</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Typical targets</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Government agencies, defense contractors, critical infrastructure</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Businesses, individuals, financial institutions</p></td>
<td style="vertical-align: top"><p>Governments, corporations, organizations opposing their views</p></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
:::
