# Configuring external inter-VM traffic with the VMware vSphere Distributed Switch (RSPAN)

:Configure network settings in VMware vCenter to allow Virtual Network Sensor to monitor data.

:::warning[Important]

The following instructions are valid as of July 2023.
:::

:::note

The following steps are for configuring the sender virtual appliance. For information about configuring the receiver virtual appliance (Virtual Network Sensor), see [Configuring external network traffic with the VMware vSphere Distributed Switch (RSPAN)](external-vsphere-switch-rspan.mdx).
:::

### Procedure {#procedure}

1.  On the VMware vCenter console, click the ![](/images/circle_icon=GUID-20230729150101.webp) tab.

2.  Right-click the name of your data center, and go to **Distributed Switch â†’ New Distributed Switch...**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM1=GUID-20230807175301.webp" />
    </figure>

3.  On the **Name and location** screen, specify the distributed switch name and location and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM2=GUID-20230807175302.webp" />
    </figure>

4.  On the **Select version** screen, select your distributed switch version and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM3=GUID-20230807175303.webp" />
    </figure>

5.  On the **Configure settings** screen, specify the port group name and the number of uplinks, and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM4=GUID-20230807175304.webp" />
    </figure>

6.  On the **Ready to complete** screen, review the settings and click **FINISH**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM5=GUID-20230807175305.webp" />
    </figure>

7.  Right-click the name of your switch and select **Add and Manage Hosts...**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM6=GUID-20230807175306.webp" />
    </figure>

8.  On the **Select task** screen, select **Add hosts** and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM7=GUID-20230807175307.webp" />
    </figure>

9.  On the **Select hosts** screen, select your virtual machine and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM8=GUID-20230807175308.webp" />
    </figure>

10. On the **Manage physical adapters** screen, right-click the name of your NIC and select **Uplink 1**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM9=GUID-20230807175309.webp" />
    </figure>

11. Click **NEXT**.

12. On the **Manage VMkernel adapters** screen, click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM10=GUID-20230807175310.webp" />
    </figure>

13. On the **Migrate VM networking** screen, click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM11=GUID-20230807175311.webp" />
    </figure>

14. On the **Ready to complete** screen, click **FINISH**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM12=GUID-20230807175312.webp" />
    </figure>

15. Click the name of your switch and click **Networks**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM13=GUID-20230807175313.webp" />
    </figure>

16. Right-click the name of your port group and select **Edit Settings...**.

17. Click **VLAN**, select **VLAN trunking** for VLAN type, and click **OK**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM14=GUID-20230807175314.webp" />
    </figure>

18. Click the ![](/images/VMware_vCenter_server_icon=GUID-20230329175050.webp) tab and then click the name of your client.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM15=GUID-20230807175315.webp" />
    </figure>

19. Click ![](/images/edit_settings_icon=GUID-20230729150707.webp).

20. On the **Edit Settings** screen, click **Network adapter 1** and select **Browse...**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM16=GUID-20230807175316.webp" />
    </figure>

21. Click the name of your port group and click **OK**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM17=GUID-20230807175317.webp" />
    </figure>

22. Click **OK**.

23. Click the ![](/images/circle_icon=GUID-20230729150101.webp) tab and then click the name of your switch.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM18=GUID-20230807175318.webp" />
    </figure>

24. Click **Configure**, click **Port Mirroring**, and click **NEW...**.

25. On the **Select session type** screen, select **Remote Mirroring Source** and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM19=GUID-20230807175319.webp" />
    </figure>

26. On the **Edit properties** screen, select **Enabled** for the status and enter your encapsulation VLAN ID for the destination VDS.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM20=GUID-20230807175320.webp" />
    </figure>

27. Click **NEXT**.

28. On the **Select sources** screen, select the virtual appliance you want to be monitored and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM21=GUID-20230807175321.webp" />
    </figure>

29. On the **Select destinations** screen, select the uplink that you bound your NIC to and click **ADD**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/External_VM2VM22=GUID-20230807175322.webp" />
    </figure>

30. Click **NEXT**.

31. On the **Ready to complete screen**, review the settings and click **FINISH**.

32. Configure the port mirror on your physical switch.

    :::warning[Important]

    Do not unpack the VLAN settings.
    :::

    After configuring your network settings, access the Virtual Network Sensor console and use the command `show traffic` to verify the Virtual Network Sensor is receiving traffic. For more information about troubleshooting, see [Virtual Network Sensor FAQ](virtual-network-sensor-faqs.mdx) and [Virtual Network Sensor CLI commands](virtual-network-sensor-cli-commands.mdx).
