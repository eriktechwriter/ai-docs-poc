### Procedure {#procedure}

1.  [Search the quarantine](searching-quarantine.mdx).

2.  Perform the following operations.

    <table>
    <colgroup>
    <col style="width: 25%" />
    <col style="width: 75%" />
    </colgroup>
    <thead>
    <tr>
    <th><p>Action</p></th>
    <th><p>Description</p></th>
    </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Restore an email message or file</p></td>
    <td><p>Select the check box of one or several items on the left side of each row, and then click <strong>Restore</strong>. The email messages or files are allowed to pass to the user.</p></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Download an email message or file in Office 365</p></td>
    <td><ol>
    <li><p>Select the check box of an item on the left side of the row, and then click <strong>Download</strong>.</p>
    <p>A confirmation screen appears.</p>
    

    :::note
    
    <p>A maximum of 10 email messages or files can be downloaded at a time.</p>
    

    :::

    </li>
    <li><p>Click <strong>OK</strong>. The email message or file is downloaded through the web browser, with a file name in the following format to better distinguish multiple downloads: &lt;timestamp&gt;_&lt;email subject or file name&gt;_&lt;affected user's name&gt;.</p>
    <p>If multiple items are selected, the information of the item with the latest timestamp will be used as the downloaded file name.</p>
    

    :::note
    
    <p>For some quarantined items that Cloud Email and Collaboration Protection thinks to have contained malicious content, you can optionally select the <strong>Use a password to protect the downloaded file</strong> check box, type a password and confirm it in the <strong>Password</strong> and <strong>Confirm password</strong> text boxes for security reasons.</p>
    <p>If multiple items are selected, you will be asked to set a password before downloading when there is at least one item considered as malicious.</p>
    

    :::

    </li>
    </ol></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Download a file in Box, Dropbox, or Google Drive</p></td>
    <td><ol>
    <li><p>Select the check box of an item on the left side of the row, and then click <strong>Download</strong>.</p>
    <p>A confirmation screen appears.</p></li>
    <li><p>Click <strong>OK</strong>. You will be redirected to the corresponding service console.</p>
    

    :::note
    
    <p>Make sure you have already logged on with your administrator credentials of the service, and then click <strong>Download</strong> on the console to download the file(s).</p>
    

    :::

    </li>
    </ol></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Download an email message in Gmail</p></td>
    <td><ol>
    <li><p>Select the check box of an item on the left side of the row, and then click <strong>Download</strong>.</p>
    <p>A confirmation screen appears.</p>
    

    :::note
    
    <p>One Gmail message can be downloaded at a time.</p>
    

    :::

    </li>
    <li><p>Click <strong>OK</strong>.</p></li>
    </ol></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Delete an email message or file</p></td>
    <td><p>Select the check box of one or several items on the left side of each row, and then click <strong>Delete</strong>. The email messages or files are purged from the Quarantine. They cannot be restored.</p></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Delete all email messages older than a specific number of days</p></td>
    <td><p>Specify the number of days to keep quarantined items within that period. Any quarantined messages or files older than the specified period automatically purge.</p></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
    <td><p>Sort email messages or files</p></td>
    <td><p>Sort the quarantined email messages or files in ascending or descending order in either of the following ways:</p>
    <ul>
    <li><p>Click the title area of either column as necessary.</p></li>
    <li><p>Click the down arrow at the right of the title area of either column, and then click <strong>Sort Ascending</strong> or <strong>Sort Descending</strong> as necessary.</p></li>
    </ul>
    <p>To cancel the current sorting, click the title area of another column to re-sort the items, or click the down arrow at the right of the title area and then click <strong>Remove Sort</strong>.</p>
    <p>To hide a column, click the down arrow at the right of its title area, and then click <strong>Hide Column</strong>.</p>
    <p>To unhide a hidden column, click the title area of another column.</p></td>
    </tr>
    </tbody>
    </table>
