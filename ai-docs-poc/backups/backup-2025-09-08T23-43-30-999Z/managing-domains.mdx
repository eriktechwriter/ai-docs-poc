::: {role="main"}
Use the **Domains** screen to add, modify, or delete domains.

To go to the **Dashboard** screen, choose **Email and Collaboration Security Operations → Cloud Email Gateway Protection → Domain**.

**Fields on the **Domains** screen**

<table>
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 22%" />
<col style="width: 78%" />
</colgroup>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Field</th>
<th>Description</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><p><strong>Domain name</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Name of a domain you added.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><p><strong>Inbound Servers</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Recipient: Recipient can be a wildcard (*) or an exact email address.</p>
<p>IP address or FQDN: Fully qualified domain name (FQDN) is a unique name, which includes both host name and domain name, and resolves to a single IP address.</p>
<ul>
<li><p>For example: <code>hostmaster1.example.com</code> or <code>mailhost.example.com</code></p></li>
<li><p>Not valid: <code>example.com</code></p></li>
</ul>
<p>Port: Port is a number from 1 to 65535 that an inbound server listens on. These ports vary based on server configuration.</p>
<p>Preference: Preference, sometimes referred to as distance, is a value from 1 to 100. The lower the preference value, the higher the priority.</p>


:::note

<p>If more than one mail server is available, delivery is prioritized to servers with lower values. Using the same value will balance delivery to each server.</p>


:::

</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><p><strong>Outbound Servers</strong></p></td>
<td><p>If outbound protection is enabled, this is the information for the MTA(s) that Cloud Email Gateway Protection relays your outbound messages from.</p>
<p>The following options are available:</p>
<p><strong>Microsoft 365</strong>: Relays your outbound messages from your Microsoft 365 solution.</p>
<p><strong>Google Workspace</strong>: Relays your outbound messages from your Google Workspace solution.</p>
<p><strong>User-defined mail servers</strong>: Relays your outbound messages from the mail servers you specified for your managed domain.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><p><strong>Time Added</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Time when a domain was added.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><p><strong>Status</strong></p></td>
<td><p>Status of a domain, which can be one of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>Completed</strong>: All required information and operations have been completed. The domain is successfully added.</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Configuration Required</strong>: Certain required information or configurations are missing or incorrect.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
