:::: {role="main"}
Configure internal network traffic with the VMware vSphere Distributed Switch using promiscuous mode to allow Virtual Network Sensor to monitor data.

:::warning[Important]

The following instructions are valid as of July 2023.
:::

### Procedure {#procedure}

1.  Turn off your Virtual Network Sensor.

2.  On the VMware vCenter console, click the ![](/images/circle_icon=GUID-20230729150101.webp) tab.

3.  Right-click the name of your switch, and then select **Distributed Port Group** and **New Distributed Port Group**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom1=GUID-20230729143531.webp" />
    </figure>

4.  On the **Name and location** screen, specify the port group name and location and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom2=GUID-20230729143532.webp" />
    </figure>

5.  On the **Configure settings** screen, view the default settings and click **NEXT**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom3=GUID-20230729143533.webp" />
    </figure>

6.  On the **Ready to complete** screen, review the settings and click **FINISH**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom4=GUID-20230729143534.webp" />
    </figure>

7.  Click the name of your switch.

8.  Click the **Networks** tab, right-click your port group name, and select **Edit Settings...**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom5=GUID-20230729143535.webp" />
    </figure>

9.  Click **Security**, select **Accept** for promiscuous mode, and click **OK**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom6=GUID-20230729143536.webp" />
    </figure>

10. Click the ![](/images/VMware_vCenter_server_icon=GUID-20230329175050.webp) tab and click the name of your virtual machine.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom7=GUID-20230729143537.webp" />
    </figure>

11. Click ![](/images/edit_settings_icon=GUID-20230729150707.webp).

12. On the **Edit Settings** screen, click **Network adapter 2** and select **Browse...**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom8=GUID-20230729143538.webp" />
    </figure>

13. Click the name of your port group and click **OK**.

    <figure>
    <img src="./images/Internal_VDS_Prom9=GUID-20230729143539.webp" />
    </figure>

14. Click **OK**.

15. Turn on your Virtual Network Sensor.

    After configuring your network settings, access the Virtual Network Sensor console and use the command `show traffic` to verify the Virtual Network Sensor is receiving traffic. For more information about troubleshooting, see [Virtual Network Sensor FAQ](virtual-network-sensor-faqs.mdx) and [Virtual Network Sensor CLI commands](virtual-network-sensor-cli-commands.mdx).
